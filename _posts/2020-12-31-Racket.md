CS 135 is a first year computer science class at the University of Waterloo. It is taught using Racket (subset of Scheme) and focuses on recursion.

Topics covered included: structure definitions, constructors, selectors, data definitions, type predicates, functions for mixed data, lists, multi-clausal recursive data definitions, trees, design methods for complex data, encapsulation, functional abstraction, filters, polymorphism, gereic functions, parametric data defintions, functions as data, functions that produce functions, anonymous functions, abstract list and tree functions, generative versus structural recursion, backtracking algorithms, accumulators, accumulative recursion, history of computer science. (Paraphrased from the <a href="https://cs.uwaterloo.ca//current/courses/course_descriptions/cDescr/CS135">Course Website</a>)
