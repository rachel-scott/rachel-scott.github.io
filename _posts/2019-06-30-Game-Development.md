My first ever computer science course was ICS3U, or Grade 11 Computer Science. 
Prior to this class I had never even published a "Hello World" program, but I enjoyed my experience so much that I went on to take ICS4U as well as CS 135, CS 136, and CS 234 in University as part of my Computing minor.

ICS3U was taught using ActionScript and Flash, and focused on game development. 
We began by learning the basics of programming - setting up files, declaring variables / functions / arrays, running the program, etc. 

The assignments got continuously more complex, until we were fully recreating some of the most popular classic games: Brick Breaker, Pong, Bubble Shooter, Space Invaders, Super Mario, and for my summative project I built Pac-Man.
The games were complete with full visuals, sound effects, and completely playable.

While ActionScript is not as popular these days, many of the concepts such as Event Listeners and Hit Test Objects, can easily be transfered to JavaScript.
